Introduction

  Earthquakes occur when two tectonic plates under the earths surface move and collide with each other causing massive 
  shakes on land that are measured in ‘magnitudes. Now soil liquefication is a phenomenon that takes place when water 
  saturated in lose granules of sand is shaken up due to the seismic activities caused by the earthquake. This shaking 
  affects the water pressure in the soil, which reduces the contact between the soil particles, which loosens soils 
  strength, and in the long term affects the foundation of all infrastructure putting billions of lives at risk. This 
  is why many civil engineers are taking active action in finding out how to correct this issue for future projects and
  developments. Civil engineers stress that it is necessary to consider soil deformations and hence, how stresses and 
  strains are redistributed through the structure.
